The Challenge

Too many children lose school to poverty.

In Rwanda, children from the poorest families are often forced out of the classroom — not because they cannot learn, but because their families cannot afford the basics. Once out of school, many end up on the streets, and the cycle repeats.

  • Tuition, uniforms, and materials families cannot afford
  • Hunger, and homes that are unstable or unsafe
  • Street life, dropout, and lost years of learning
The children Barere serves
What We Do

A whole-child model that keeps children in school.

We do not just get children back into class — we build the home and community conditions that let them stay.

Education Support

Tuition, notebooks, uniforms, supplies, and school feeding — everything a child needs to enroll, take part fully, and stay in school.

Mentorship

One-on-one check-ins and group workshops that build confidence, life skills, and hope for a future beyond hard circumstances.

Family Support

Because a child's challenges start at home, we help guardians build income and stability — including start-up funds for small businesses.

Nutrition & the "Gira Ingurube" passing-on model

Every child received a chicken for nutrition. Next, families raise pigs — and pass piglets on to new families joining Barere. Each cohort empowers the next, in a cycle of shared responsibility and community ownership.
